Augusta

A Capital Opportunity

Augusta is the state capital of Maine. It boasts a beautiful riverfront and a wealth of classic brick-built architecture. The city is also home to landmarks and educational destinations, such as Old Fort Western and the Maine State Museum. Between its importance as state capital and its attractions, Augusta is well worth a visit.

  • 18,899 Population [1]
  • Incorporated in 1849 [2]
  • Located in Kennebec County
  • Urban
  • $45,006 Median Household Income [1] ($23,245 below state avg.) [3]

Up from Humble Beginnings

A capital opportunity, indeed. Despite having a much lower population and median household income than Portland, Augusta became the new capital city of Maine in 1827 due to its prominent, central location in the state [4]. The economy in Augusta is strongly based around government and services, with 41.1% of jobs being in the service sector and 20.9% being in government [5]. These numbers make sense, with the city’s heavy involvement in government as state capital.

Augusta has something to offer tourists, too. Fort Western has been maintained to give visitors and locals alike a glimpse into the area’s rich history, and the Maine State Museum offers even more knowledge on Maine’s past. Augusta is worth visiting for anyone with a love for knowledge and history.
