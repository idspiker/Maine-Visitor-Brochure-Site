Portland

Yes. Life's Good Here

If you want to explore charming streets and an exciting waterfront, Portland is the place for you. The streets are lined with every kind of shop and restaurant you can imagine, and the waterfront is home to many storied docks and boats. Indulge in your adventurous side at every turn.

  • 68,408 Population [1]
  • Incorporated in 1832 [2]
  • Located in Cumberland County
  • Urban
  • $69,046 Median Household Income [1] ($796 above state avg.) [3]

Charming Even in Success

Portland was actually the first capital of Maine when it was established in 1832 [2]. Despite no longer being the capital of the state, the city is still a wonderful city. Downtown is bustling with life and culture, and despite the size of the city it still has a distinct charm.

Portland provides plenty of economic opportunity for the businesses that line its streets. The city has a strong tourism economy, with tourists spending over $8.6 billion in 2022 [4]. The influx of visitors and money from outside the city allows businesses to make far more than they would if they were only meeting the needs of the locals.

There is a lot to see in Portland. The bustling waterfront provides sight-seeing and exploration on its many docks. Events such as the Maine Lobster Festival and Yarmouth Clam Festival are held yearly in the city, giving locals and visitors alike even more to do [5].
