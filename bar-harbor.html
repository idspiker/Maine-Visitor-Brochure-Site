Bar Harbor

Ba Ha Ba

Bar Harbor is a hot tourist destination, boasting everything from quaint shops to atmospheric pubs. It also borders on Acadia National Park, offering peaceful enjoyment to those who wish to escape the hubbub of the crowds. Whether you are a hiker or a shopper, this place is for you!

What About Bar Harbor?

Bar Harbor’s notoriety as a summer retreat began early in its history. In the 1800s, people were already building summer cottages and hotels to host guests seeking out the beauty of the mountains and coasts [2]. Even now, almost two centuries later, the allure of the mountains brings many visitors to this resort town. In the summer and fall the tourist scene booms. Local businesses and restaurants make the bulk of their profit in the summer while cruise ships pour hundreds of visitors into Bar Harbor each day. You can buy anything from bar harbor souvenirs to fresh caught lobster at the height of the season.

The town empties out in the winter. Without the tourists to do business with, many shops close for the off season. As many people only live and work in Bar Harbor in the summer, the population also drops significantly in the winter. In the tourist season, the town’s population is over 18,000, but during the winter it is only around 5,000 [4].
